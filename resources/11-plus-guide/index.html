How to Prepare for the 11+ in Redbridge

📅 June 2026⏱ 8 min readHorizon Tuition

The 11+ examination is one of the most significant academic milestones in your child's education. In Redbridge and the surrounding area, selective grammar schools remain highly sought-after — and competition for places is fierce.

Which Schools Are We Talking About?

From Redbridge, children commonly apply to Ilford County High School for Boys, Woodford County High School for Girls, Valentines High School (partially selective), and independent selective schools such as Bancroft's.

GL Assessment vs CEM: What's the Difference?

GL Assessment papers are more structured and predictable, testing Verbal Reasoning, Non-Verbal Reasoning, Mathematics and English in separate papers. CEM papers are designed to be harder to prepare for, testing similar skills in less predictable formats.

When Should You Start?

The ideal starting point is Year 4 — giving your child a relaxed, two-year preparation. Starting in Year 5 is still very effective. Even starting at the beginning of Year 6 is not too late for an intensive programme.

What Does Preparation Involve?

  • Verbal Reasoning: word patterns, letter codes, analogies, vocabulary
  • Non-Verbal Reasoning: spatial patterns, matrices, sequences
  • Mathematics: speed and accuracy on arithmetic plus problem-solving
  • English: comprehension, vocabulary, grammar and creative writing
  • Timed practice: the 11+ is as much about speed as accuracy
