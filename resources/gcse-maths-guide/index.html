GCSE Maths: How to Move From Grade 3 to Grade 5

📅 April 2026⏱ 7 min readHorizon Tuition

Grade 4 is the GCSE standard pass. Grade 5 is the strong pass that most sixth forms require. Moving from Grade 3 to Grade 5 is entirely achievable with the right approach — extra hours on past papers alone rarely close the gap.

Why Grade 3 Students Often Get Stuck

Grade 3 students usually have shaky foundations in key areas — particularly algebra, fractions, and ratio. They focus revision time on topics they already know rather than those that would gain the most marks.

The Topics That Move Students Most

  • Fractions, decimals and percentages — high volume of marks at Foundation tier
  • Basic algebra — solving equations, rearranging formulae
  • Ratio and proportion — appears across many question types
  • Area, perimeter, volume — reliably tested every year
  • Probability — often well-rewarded with good technique

The Right Revision Approach

Don't just do past papers — diagnose first. Complete a paper under timed conditions, then categorise every lost mark by topic. That topic list is your revision plan.
