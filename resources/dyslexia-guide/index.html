What is Dyslexia and How Can Tuition Help?

📅 March 2026⏱ 6 min readHorizon Tuition

Dyslexia affects around 1 in 10 people in the UK. Many children go through years of school without ever receiving the targeted support that would make a genuine difference.

What is Dyslexia?

Dyslexia is a specific learning difficulty that primarily affects reading and spelling. It is neurological in origin and is not related to intelligence.

10 Signs Your Child Might Have Dyslexia

  • Difficulty learning to read despite good general intelligence
  • Slow, halting reading with frequent errors
  • Poor spelling that doesn't improve with practice
  • Difficulty remembering sequences (alphabet, months of the year)
  • Confusion between similar letters (b/d, p/q)
  • Reluctance to read aloud
  • Difficulty following multi-step instructions
  • Good verbal skills but poor written work
  • Family history of reading difficulties
  • Low self-esteem around reading and writing

How Specialist Tuition Helps

The most effective approaches for dyslexia are multisensory — engaging visual, auditory, and kinaesthetic learning simultaneously. The key is working at the child's actual reading level, not their chronological age.
